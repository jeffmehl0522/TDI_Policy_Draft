POSITION PAPER

THE ROLES OF ORDAINED, NON-ORDAINED, LICENSED and/or CREDENTIALED
SPIRITUAL DIRECTORS

PURPOSE:  Questions are often raised about the non-clergy role of Spiritual Directors on a weekend, as well as the role of the Spiritual Director on the local Secretariat, which this Position Paper will attempt to address. It will also attempt to address the various scenarios which go into qualifying or disqualifying someone as a Spiritual Director.

-          For additional insights, it is strongly recommended that this document be viewed in light of the following Policy Committee Documents:

o   November, 2017 Best Practice document entitled “Best Practices for Qualifying Pescadores to Serve as Spiritual Directors.”

o   November, 2017 Position Paper entitled “Role of the Clergy on the Local Secretariat.”

BACKGROUND:  The basic requirements for a Pescadore to serve as a Spiritual Director on a Tres Dias weekend, as noted in the Essentials, is that a Spiritual Director must be qualified to teach and counsel in spiritual matters by their church or denomination, and that at least one be ordained, licensed or credentialed to regularly celebrate Holy Communion by his or her church or denomination. (Sections 3.2.2 – 3.2.3).  

These basic requirements, by design, permit communities to add to the requirements in order to best meet their needs based on their demographics, traditions, denominations and churches being served, etc.  However, this vagueness also raises questions which are sometimes difficult to address given that same diversity.   

IMPORTANT: 

1)      It goes without saying that ALL team members, including Spiritual Directors, must be in agreement with the Qualifications for Community Leadership, the Tres Dias Statement of Belief and be willing to abide by the Essentials of Tres Dias. 

2)      These decisions are complicated by the fact that there are many gray areas between the roles of Clergy and Laity, and this document will attempt to address as many of those areas as possible.

3)      The document entitled “Glossary of Tres Dias Terms” equates the term “Clergy” with “Spiritual Director.”  This means that the two terms, as applied to Tres Dias, can be used interchangeably.

4)      References will be made in this document to “Leadership” roles on a weekend.  It is the position of Tres Dias International that ALL team members are considered “Leaders” in that they are Servant-Leaders for Christ.  In this document, however, the term “leader” will designate the formal leadership roles of the weekend (ie: Rector, Head Cha/Chief Aux, etc).





5)      Although this document will refer to individuals generically as “Spiritual Directors,” that phrase will serve as a “Title” or “Category,” but not necessarily the Role.  In other words, those who qualify based on meeting the necessary parameters will be referred to as Spiritual Directors (title/category), but must still be approved by a local Tres Dias community to serve at that level (Role). 

DISCUSSION: 

The following guidelines apply to all Pescadores and specifically address the definition of Spiritual Director in the Tres Dias Ministry as defined by the Essentials and the Policies & Procedures of Tres Dias International.  

It is the opinion of the Policy Committee that, as much as possible, the functions between Spiritual Director and Lay Leadership within the Tres Dias Ministry not be confused; that there is a clear delineation between that of the call and purpose of a Spiritual Director and those of the Layperson, both in the Kingdom of God and within the Tres Dias Ministry and its weekends.  We therefore issue the following parameters as they pertain to questions that are often raised:

1)      ORDAINED INDIVIDUALS ARE AUTOMATICALLY DISQUALIFIED FROM SERVING IN LAY LEADERSHIP POSITIONS:  Ordination is one of the black & white indicators of a person’s qualifications to automatically be placed in the category (title) of Spiritual Director.  However, in order to serve in the role of a Spiritual Director within the Tres Dias Ministry, they must also meet the minimum qualifications (Teaching/Counseling as per Essentials Section 3.2.2) and they must be approved by their local Secretariat.

If a person is Ordained by his or her Denomination, Church/Local Congregation, or a para-church organization, they are automatically disqualified from serving in Lay Leadership roles on a weekend or on the local Secretariat.  Assuming they meet the minimum requirements (Essentials Section 3.2.2), they are qualified to apply for the role of Spiritual Director in a community.  This applies whether or not they are active or inactive, retired or currently serving in a ministerial capacity. 

a.      ORDAINED INDIVIDUALS WHO ARE NOT QUALIFIED TO TEACH OR COUNSEL:  An ordained person who is not qualified to teach or counsel on spiritual matters (Essentials Section 3.2.2) is limited to serving in ancillary lay positions (non-Rollo Room Roles) if permitted by the local community.  (See the Summary and the section on “Community Discretion” at the end of this document).

b.      QUESTIONS RELATED TO THE VALIDITY OF A PERSON’S ORDINATION:  Given the diverse methods of ordination (everything from years of formal study and training to the simple laying-on of hands, and even internet-generated ordinations), Tres Dias is not in a position to evaluate the validity of a person’s ordination.  Tres Dias relies on the person’s local church or denomination for that validation, and will accept that credentialing. 



c.       OTHER LEVELS OF ORDINATION:  These need to be considered on a case-by-case basis.  One example is the Ordination of Deacons:  Since the role and training of Deacons is very different within the denominations, each needs to be considered separately; based on the amount of training and the functions permitted by that
church or denomination.  The Policy Committee will assist in evaluating these exceptions as necessary.

2)      NON-ORDAINED INDIVIDUALS: 

a.      PERSON QUALIFIED TO TEACH/COUNSEL IN SPIRITUAL MATTERS AND IS OR HAS ACTIVELY SERVED IN THOSE ROLES ARE DISQUALIFIED FROM SERVING AS LAITY:  If a person is qualified by his or her Denomination, Church/Local Congregation to teach and counsel in spiritual matters (Essentials Section 3.2.2), AND they are currently serving, or have served in those roles, they qualify for the category (title) of “Spiritual Director” and may apply to the local community to serve in the role of Spiritual Director on a weekend.  They are, however, disqualified from serving as laity within Tres Dias.  This applies whether or not they are active or inactive, retired or currently serving in a ministerial capacity.

b.      PERSON QUALIFIED TO TEACH AND COUNSEL BUT IS NOT AND HAS NOT ACTIVELY SERVED IN THOSE ROLES ARE CONSIDERED LAITY:  If a person (Paid or Volunteer) is qualified by his or her Denomination, Church/Local Congregation, to teach and counsel in spiritual matters (meeting the requirements of Essentials Section 3.2.2), but is not and has not served in those roles, they are considered laity and may continue serving in any lay positions on a weekend or in the local Secretariat.    

                                                              i.      In the event that they begin to provide teaching/counseling services in their church or denomination, they will then automatically fall into the general category (Title) of Spiritual Director and can no longer serve in Lay positions.

c.       PERSON SERVING IN A ROLE THAT IS CONSIDERED BY THEIR CHURCH OR DENOMINATION TO BE CLERGY OR MINISTERIAL ARE CONSIDERED TO BE IN THE GENERAL CATEGORY OF SPIRITUAL DIRECTOR:  If a person (Paid or Volunteer) is serving their church or denomination in a role that is considered to be Clergy or Ministerial and are viewed as part of the Pastoral/Ministerial staff (Pastor, Evangelist, Preacher, Teaching Pastor, Visiting Pastor, Chaplain, Youth/College/Children’s Pastor, etc.) whether ordained/credentialed or not, they fall within the category/title of Spiritual Director and are qualified to apply for the role, assuming they meet the qualifications of Essentials Section 3.2.2 (Teaching/Counseling).  They may not, however, serve as laity on weekends or on the local Secretariat.

                                                              i.      To be clear, this does not include those who teach Sunday School classes or serve as teachers/directors/volunteers for youth or children’s classes or departments, or as praise and worship leaders, or Elders/Deacons who occasionally fill in for their pastors, etc., unless they are otherwise qualified per Section 3.2.2. 



3)      “ONCE A SPIRITUAL DIRECTOR, ALWAYS A SPIRITUAL DIRECTOR:” If a person meets the minimum requirements to be a Spiritual Director on a Tres Dias weekend as noted in the Essentials, (i.e. qualified to teach and counsel in spiritual matters - Section 3.2.2) and has served as a Spiritual Director in Tres Dias or any equivalent movement, that person is a Spiritual Director and cannot return to the role of laity.  

a.      In addition, if a person has served as a Spiritual Director in one community, that person may not serve as laity in any other community.

SUMMARY:  Therefore, in keeping with the principle that Tres Dias is a Lay Led Movement, a person who falls into the above parameters and is considered to hold the Title of Spiritual Director by Tres Dias International, even if not serving in that role, MAY NOT serve in the following positions, or comparable ones as may be used by various communities, on a Tres Dias Team/Weekend:

-          Rector

-          Head Cha or Chief Auxiliary

-          Assistant Head Cha or Auxiliary

-          Section Head or Assistant Head Section Leader

-          Lay Rollista/Silent Professor/Table Leader

o   Spiritual Directors are prohibited from serving in any role at a table on the weekend since the essence of the discussions are to be “lay led.”  Having a Spiritual Director helping to lead the discussion would go against the spirit, if not the letter, of that principle.

-          In addition, Spiritual Directors are only permitted to serve consulting roles on the local Secretariat.  They are not permitted to hold elected positions or to have voting status.  (See the November, 2017 Position Paper entitled “Role of Clergy on the Local Secretariat” for additional insights).

PERMISSIBLE EXCEPTIONS TO THE ABOVE GUIDELINES:

1)      A person who graduates from seminary but has not taken on a formal ministerial role is considered laity until that point in time when they assume the ministerial function(s) noted above.

2)      Although we have attempted to address as many of the ‘gray’ areas as possible, it’s virtually impossible to cover every potential scenario.  Given the diversity of how various roles are viewed by different churches and denominations, these may have to be considered on an individual basis if questions arise, and the Policy Committee will be available to help in their determination as needed.

3)      COMMUNITY DISCRETION:  Although the Policy Committee suggests that those who serve communities as Spiritual Directors be utilized only as Spiritual Directors on a weekend, a community may, at its discretion, permit clergy to serve in ancillary, non-leadership lay roles, such as a Cha or Auxiliary. 



We do, however, encourage communities to permit those who fall within the generic title of “Spiritual Director” as defined above, but who are not qualified to serve in that role, be permitted to serve in ancillary positions.  They may not, however, serve in any of the leadership roles noted in the Summary above.

In addition, communities are encouraged to add additional qualifications for a person to be a Spiritual Director based upon their needs and demographics in an effort to get the highest level of clergy possible in their area.  They may not, however, lessen the qualifications as outlined in this document or in the Essentials.  To avoid conflicts, communities are also encouraged to have a specific process/procedure for vetting Spiritual Directors to assure transparency and equity when determining if a pescadore meets their qualifications to serve as a Spiritual Director.

GRANDFATHERING:  Our suggestion is that those Spiritual Directors who have been faithfully and effectively serving in that role be permitted to continue even if their qualifications do not match those listed above, but this will be left to the local community to decide.  Moving forward, however, all communities are expected to adhere to the above qualifications for new Spiritual Directors.
